Research Programme & Vision
My research programme addresses a fundamental challenge for perioperative and critical care medicine: how do we sustain nursing workforces, optimise real-time clinical decision-making, and deliver safe, equitable care in increasingly complex, technology-intensive surgical environments?
This programme aligns with the NIHR Imperial BRC's four strategic threads: Digital Health, Early Diagnosis, Precision Medicine, and Convergence Science.
1. Real-Time Intervention for Surgical Nursing Workforce Sustainability
My PflegeStressPlan (PSP) research programme at Charité addresses nursing workforce sustainability in high-acuity surgical environments. The STERN-57 validation (N=447 nurses) is the first international study to apply Exploratory Structural Equation Modelling to nursing stress measurement—demonstrating methodological leadership. Parallel diary-elicited qualitative research with ICU nurses identifies structural levers for healthy work environments in surgical settings. Building on this foundation, I am developing the IN-SYNC study—a Just-In-Time Adaptive Intervention using smartphone-based ecological momentary assessment to deliver personalised micro-interventions addressing nursing stress during clinical shifts in surgical and critical care environments.
2. Digital Health, AI Ethics & Clinical Decision Support
My work on speculative ethics and healthcare algorithmics (published in Nursing Philosophy) provides frameworks for evaluating how AI-augmented diagnostics in ICU/perioperative pathways reproduce inequalities and reshape clinical judgment. This connects directly to Imperial BRC's Digital Health theme. I have published on AI ethics for nursing in OJIN (2025) and hold a Carl Duisberg Award (€20,000) for research on 'Medicine, Nursing and Healthcare in the 4th Industrial Revolution'.
3. Critical Posthuman Perspectives on Life Support Technologies
I bring unique theoretical expertise—grounded in 15 years' frontline transplant and critical care practice—to the ethical complexities of technologically intensive care. My forthcoming work on the necropolitics of ECMO (Duke University Press, 2026) examines how extracorporeal life support technologies reconfigure thresholds of life and death. I have developed Posthuman Institutional Ethnography (PIE) as methodology for understanding human-technology assemblages in clinical settings (Journal of Advanced Nursing, 2025). This stream addresses urgent questions about simulation replacing clinical placement, algorithmic clinical decision support, and nursing's role in increasingly automated care environments—directly relevant to Imperial's technology-intensive surgical and critical care pathways.
4. Patient Safety, Human Factors & Antimicrobial Stewardship
I have taught Crew Resource Management and Human Factors in Critical Care at Charité, and contribute to the SHORTER trial examining antibiotic stewardship in critical care. This connects to Imperial BRC's Infection & Antimicrobial Resistance theme.
Strategic Fit with Imperial
I offer Imperial a rare combination: substantive transplant surgery and critical care clinical expertise with continuous practice across the full transplant pathway; methodological range spanning advanced psychometrics to qualitative inquiry; and theoretical sophistication positioning nursing research as a contributor to convergence science. My clinical background in liver, kidney, and pancreas transplantation aligns directly with Imperial's hepatobiliary and transplant surgery programmes at Hammersmith Hospital. I hold an existing honorary NHS contract through NHS Lothian's Clinical Academic Home framework, demonstrating established experience bridging academic research and NHS clinical practice. I am prepared to establish an honorary clinical contract with Imperial College Healthcare NHS Trust, lead NMAHPP research capacity building complementing the existing HPAG infrastructure, contribute to the Imperial BRC, and shape the new MRes in Perioperative & Critical Care.

PI-Led Research Programmes
PflegeStressPlan (PSP) – Principal Investigator
"Nursing Stress in Germany: Mixed-Methods Research Programme"
2022–present | Institutional core funding, Charité – Institute of Gender in Medicine
Independent research programme developed through institutional investment, addressing Germany's nursing crisis (Pflegenotstand) – 500,000+ additional nurses needed by 2030, >50% reporting chronic stress. This programme provides the evidence base for planned external funding applications (DFG Eigene Stelle, IN-SYNC study).
Programme Components:
* STERN-57 Validation Study (N=447 nurses, Berlin hospitals): First German validation of Extended Nursing Stress Scale; first international ESEM validation (methodological innovation). Under review, International Journal of Nursing Studies
* ICU Structural Stress Study (N=10 diary-elicited interviews): Qualitative study of structural strain and adaptive practice in intensive care. Reflexive thematic analysis using Job Demands-Resources framework. Targeted at Journal of Advanced Nursing Special Issue 'Healthy Work Environments'
* Systematic Review: German nurse wellbeing interventions – synthesis of intervention evidence
* Methodological Innovation: Mobile diary methods and ecological momentary assessment for nursing research
Key Outputs: Validated German stress instrument; actionable levers for healthy work environments (structured break coverage, ethical triage norms, IEN-tailored onboarding, transparent staffing ratios); novel methodology for studying nursing work in real-time.
